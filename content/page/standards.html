--- title: "Standards" ---

The goal is to produce standards that compliment, and where relevant replace, existing industry standards such as ISO 27001:2013.

ESC standards are being governed by a process called Ethereum Opsec Proposals (EOPs) and managed in the EOP Github repository. The process is defined by EOP-1.

All standards should first be considered as an Ethereum Improvement Proposal (EIP) and only proposals that are out of scope for EIP should be submitted as EOP.

Below is a brief summary of relevant EIPs and EOPs with respective status.

EIP-679

EIP: (to be assigned)
Link: https://github.com/ethereum/EIPs/pull/679
Title: Ethereum Vulnerability Reporting Framework
Type: Informational
Status: Draft
Created: 2017-07-21

The goal of this framework, and the reason for standardization, is to offer users a consistent and reliable disclosure process that will improve responsiveness, information accuracy and ultimately security of the ecosystem as a whole.

EIP-evss

EIP: (to be assigned)
Link: (not yet submitted)
Title: Ethereum Vulnerability Scoring System
Type: Informational
Status: Idea
Created: 2017-08-06

The scope of this standard is to come with an alternative to the Common Vulnerability Scoring System (CVSS) that is adapted to Ethereum and the use case around smart contract etc.

Certification

ESC is working on an auditing and certification process for projects and organizations that want to prove adherence to ESC standards.
